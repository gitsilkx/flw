holidays to menorca

Menorca is one of the most famous tourist destinations belonging to Spain. Located on the Mediterranean Sea and belonging to the enchanting Balearic Islands, Menorca is a small island setting and thus home to the ultimate vacation experience with its peaceful setting in contrast to its neighboring island of Ibiza. Its less crowded beaches offer tranquility to those wishing to go on holidays to Menorca. The Mount Toro offers an interesting hike for one to explore the north of this island. The capital of Menorca i.e. Mahon is relatively more happening than the serene beaches and mountains it is home to. The predominant form of architecture in most of its ancient buildings and monuments is the Baroque form, one such exemplary monument which showcases this is the Ciutadella. The Punta Prima is a famous resort, visited by a number of tourists annually for its extraordinary beaches and is located towards the south-east of this island. The longest beach on this soothing island setting is the Sou Pou where one can engage in a number of water sports. The Cap de Favatrix is another beach like area, located towards the north which offers undulating hiking tracks and an array of natural beauty.

Menorca is thus home to a number of fun-filled and exploration activities, such as scuba diving, swimming and snorkeling at the Bay of Son Xoriguer and Port Addaia. Spanish cuisine is widely displayed at various restaurants, bars and pubs all over Menorca. Some of the most enjoyed representations of Spanish cuisine include lobster stew, calderata de Ilagosta and Ensemada cakes. The fish markets of the Mahon’s Placa Espanya offer a various fresh water fish species. Music is an important component of Menorcan lifestyle and is manifested through various live performances such as those at the Jazz Club in San Clement. Coupled with international DJ’s and a party-loving crown, Menorcan clubs are open up till late in the night and offer a thrilling clubbing experience. Menorca is renowned for its summer fiestas which are a source of enjoyment for all who are on holidays to Menorca. ‘Festes de Sant Juan’ is a traditional festival filled with parades of sheep and black horses during the day alongside celebrations and bonfires during the night. The festival comprises of three days and is concluded with a competition between various acclaimed horse riders of Menorca, following which an impressive firework celebration brings the marvelous fiesta to an end.
